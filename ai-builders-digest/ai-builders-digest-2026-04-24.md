过度烹饪 / overcooking

你见过这个：有人发布了一个仪表板，每个数字都有迷你图，每个操作都有确认弹窗，每个空状态都有动画插图和标语。单独看，每个决定对某个人来说都有道理。但合在一起就感觉是混乱的。没有重点。/ you've seen this: someone ships a dashboard that shows every number with a sparkline, every action has a confirmation modal, every empty state has an animated illustration and a tagline. individually each decision made sense to someone. together it feels like chaos. nothing is in focus.

这就是过度烹饪。不是单个决策在孤立情况下是坏的，而是多个合理的决策累积起来，没有人对其说不。/ that's overcooking. not one bad decision in isolation, but the accumulation of reasonable ones that no one said no to.

AI 让这变得更糟，因为添加功能的成本降到了接近零。它可以在几分钟内构建一个功能，甚至是一个全新的概念。所以人们就做了。然后再做一次。原本目标明确的东西慢慢变成了一堆添加物的集合，每个都看似合理，但整体却缺乏连贯性。/ AI makes this worse as the cost of adding dropped to near zero. it can build a feature, even a whole new concept in minutes. so people do. and then they do it again. the thing that started with a clear purpose slowly becomes a collection of additions that are each justifiable but collectively incoherent.

根本问题是，大多数"新想法"并不是真正新的。它们只是已经存在于更基础层面的东西的重新包装。一个新标签贴在了旧概念上。因为有东西变了，有新词和新皮肤——感觉像是进步，但思考并没有深入，只是复制成了混乱。/ the root problem is that most "new ideas" aren't new. they're repackaging of something that already exists at a more fundamental level. a new sticker on an old concept. it feels like progress because something changed, with a new word and skin – but the thinking didn't go deeper, it just duplicated itself into confusion.

整体有一个核心。一旦你理解了整个系统，你就能感受到它。一切都相互关联且平衡。当你过度加载它时，那种引力就减弱了。不是因为任何单一的东西是错的——而是因为注意力是有限的，你把它分散到了各处。/ the whole has a core. you feel it once you understand the whole system. everything in it are related and balanced. when you overload it, that gravity weakens. not because any one thing is wrong – but because attention is finite and you force it everywhere.

我们需要的不是更多制造垃圾的工具。是看穿混乱，回到事物本身，砍掉一切不服务于它的东西。这比以往更难，而不是更容易。因为总有什么可以用一个提示再添加进去。/ what we need aren't more tools that make more slop. it's seeing through the chaos, and returning to what the thing actually is, and cutting everything that doesn't serve that. that's harder now, not easier. because there's always something else you could add with one more prompt.
